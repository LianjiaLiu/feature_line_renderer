Introduction

Story


In the quest for a lost city, M stumbled upon an astonishing find—an active transmitter hidden within the ruins. Caught between reporting the discovery and succumbing to the allure of personal exploration, M grappled with a decision poised to reshape destiny. The dance of caution and curiosity heightened as M envisioned the mysteries on the other side.

Unbeknownst to him, a mysterious counterpart, M*, awaited, ready to claim not just M's life but his identity.

Conception


Step into the immersive world of the scene design, where three pivotal elements—feature line, nuanced lighting, and evocative smoke—converge to craft a cinematic scene.

The interplay of two worlds unfolds through carefully curated lighting tones, character textures, and distinct backgrounds. Meanwhile, the gaze is skillfully guided towards the central character through the artful use of blooming and rim lights which delicately outlines characters. And the dramatic presence of smoke, reminiscent of gunpowder, hints at an unknown battle.

04

Scene Design

The entire experience can be broken down into two stages:
1) ideation and placement of 3D models
2) arranging lighting and smoke

The objective of this design is to simulate a cinematic shot or poster, inspired by the idea of depicting two worlds—one with feature line and the other without. However it's challenging for me to blend elements with different shading techniques naturally.

An alternative, more feasible approach involves portraying two worlds under the same shader but with distinct color settings, including character appearances. This idea is inspired by Yudhistira who also helps manipulate the poses and the reverse texture. Different backgrounds are also introduced to signify the unique identities of two characters.

Regarding lighting, to evoke a cinematic ambiance, I turned to Pixar's lighting tutorial and implemented some basic techniques. For example, approximately 8 point lights were carefully placed as rim lights, outlining characters against the background. The lighting for the evil version drew inspiration from various shots in the Spider-Verse.

Smoke also plays a pivotal role in the design. Even with proper lighting, the scene lacks dynamism without smoke, as everything appears somewhat static and lacks a storytelling element. Smoke becomes instrumental in infusing life and narrative depth into the composition.

Features

01

Physically-based Feature Line

My teammate and I share a keen interest in non-photo-realistic rendering, leading us to focus on incorporating feature lines as a core element in our project. Based on the method introduced in Physically-based Feature Line Rendering by Rex West, a line detector is integrated into the pathtracer. It turns out that the feature line results in a significant transformation of the scene's ambiance.

However, there are some other issues we did not expect after implementing feature line.

1. Line detector requires more efforts in parameter tuning when working with halton sampler thus we ended up using independent sampler for the base integrator and halton for albedo and normal integrator during denoising.

2. Feature line detection largely scales up the number of samples, ie. final samples = samples * line_samples. We get in short budget for the number of samples so in the end denoising becomes a must.

03

Smoke

To seamlessly merge two worlds, heterogeneous smoke is attributed significant credit to. However, no heterogeneous participating medium is implemented. Multiple alpha masks are used to filter rays, generating noisy occluded results that simulate smoke. Based on specific pixel values, the ray has a certain probability of being blocked (credits to Alex's hints on this).

While transparency and heterogeneity are achieved with alpha masks, additional depth noise is vital for a fuller sense of volumetrics. This is simulated through multiple layers of masks along the depth.

Importantly, the use of alpha masks significantly reduces computational costs for rendering heterogeneous smoke. In my final scene, five masks create the impression of smoke in a container, hinted at by tracks in the corners.
